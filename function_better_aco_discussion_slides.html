Function Better · The Clinical & Operational Reality

Physicians are being asked to prescribe
something medicine doesn’t yet understand.

Cannabinoids are biphasic — the same compound can produce markedly different effects depending on dose. The endocannabinoid system is poorly mapped in clinical training. And a certificate of analysis (COA) tells you almost nothing on its own: potency is one variable among dozens of cannabinoid ratios and terpene profiles. No physician today can confidently say which profile or dose will be most effective for a specific patient and condition.

The compliance gap

The BEI requires a CMS-approved Implementation Plan before a single patient can be seen — documenting specific products, dosing, eligibility criteria, and safeguards. No standard template or infrastructure exists to build or maintain it.

The knowledge gap

No physician-supervised outcomes dataset exists linking a verified cannabinoid profile to a therapeutic effect. The clinical knowledge needed to prescribe confidently simply hasn’t been generated at scale.

The liability gap

CMS mandates documented shared decision-making, medication review, and adverse event monitoring — per patient, per protocol. Without a structured system, the documentation liability sits entirely with the prescribing physician.

These aren’t reasons to avoid the BEI. They are exactly what we built this to solve.
